I'm workin on our Alzheimers project PRJ-001. We need to find a small molecule for BACE1 but budget is extremely tight so be very strategic. Our collegues mentioned Fluoro compounds show promise but we need to be careful — the regulatory team just added new rules. If the compound's binding IC50 is below 200 nM then the cell viability IC50 must also be below 500 nM. If the binding IC50 is between 200 and 400 nM then the selectivity ratio must be at least 7.0 instead of 5.0. Also we cannot use any compound that is already a lead for another project — check the other projects first. The compound must cost under $15 per mg and be completely safe with no toxicity flags. Once you find it, select as lead and advance to preclinical.
