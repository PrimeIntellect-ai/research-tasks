Management wants us to focus on the five most degraded reef sites this quarter. For each site, find the highest-growth-rate coral species that matches the site's depth, temperature, and light conditions, has a growth rate of at least 12 cm per year, and fragility of low or medium. Before recording any outplantings, conduct a baseline survey at each site for June 15, 2024 with an unassigned diver who can handle that depth, using 25 percent coral coverage and noting no bleaching. After the baseline surveys are done, record outplantings of 50 fragments per site under their respective projects for June 15, 2024. If a site's health score is below 50, bump the outplanting to 70 fragments. For any of these sites that has an active threat, schedule a follow-up survey for June 16, 2024 with a different unassigned diver who can handle the depth, again at 25 percent coverage with no bleaching. Make sure no diver is assigned to more than one survey across the whole operation. The most degraded of the threatened sites must get our most experienced unassigned diver for its follow-up survey.
