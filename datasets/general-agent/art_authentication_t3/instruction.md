Our museum is conducting a comprehensive authentication review of a recently acquired collection of over fifty paintings attributed to seven major Impressionist and Post-Impressionist artists. This is one of the most complex reviews we've undertaken — intelligence suggests some of these works may be sophisticated forgeries with fabricated provenance documents and carefully replicated signatures. For each artwork, you must check the provenance, examine the signature, and analyze the materials at minimum.

Critical authentication rules you must follow exactly: if materials analysis reveals any anachronistic pigment or binding agent, the work MUST be authenticated as a forgery regardless of how strong the provenance or signature appears — material evidence trumps all other indicators. An inconsistent signature also indicates forgery. However, an absent signature is NOT automatically suspicious — many artists including Degas occasionally left works unsigned. Weak provenance alone, especially when attributed to wartime document loss, is NOT sufficient grounds for rejection if both the signature (or its absence is explained) and materials are consistent. For works where only some evidence is available, you must weigh the available indicators carefully.

Please authenticate every artwork in the system as either genuine or a forgery.
