I'm organizing a five-show international fashion week and need you to set everything up from scratch. The shows are Paris Spring Gala (Elena Voss, haute-couture), Tokyo Street Week (Marco Silva, streetwear), Berlin Avant-Garde Night (Yuki Tanaka, avant-garde), Milan Classic Elegance (Claire Dupont, classic), and Mumbai Street Vibe (Raj Kapoor, streetwear).

For each show, you need to pick a venue, set the date, assign a sponsor, cast one model, and give them an outfit from that show's designer. The dates should be: Paris and Berlin on March 15th, Tokyo and Mumbai on April 10th, and Milan on April 25th. Every venue must be different, and every sponsor must be different too.

Now the tricky part — there are several rules that interact with each other. Haute-couture shows always need a model with runway experience (runway or both specialty). No model can walk in two shows on the same date. The total model budget across all five shows is $19,000. If a venue costs $20,000 or more, the model for that show has to be $5,000 or below. Each sponsor needs to be silver tier or above, and their budget has to cover the show's venue cost plus the model's rate. Here's the sponsor-specific part: if a sponsor is platinum tier, the model must have runway or both specialty regardless of the show type; and if a sponsor is gold tier, the model's rate has to be $4,500 or below. Don't reuse any outfit across shows.
