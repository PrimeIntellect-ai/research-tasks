4. A protein's total spectrum is the sum of the peaks of all its constituent amino acids.

1. Parse the FASTA file `/home/user/proteins.fasta` to get the 5 protein sequences. Ensure they are processed in the order they appear in the file.
2. Generate the simulated spectrum for each of the 5 proteins.
3. Use a non-negative least squares optimization (e.g., `scipy.optimize.nnls`) to find the weights of the 5 proteins that best fit the experimental spectrum `/home/user/exp_spectrum.npy`.

6. Save the results in a JSON file at `/home/user/results.json` with exactly the following keys:
   - `"weights"`: A list of the 5 estimated weights (floats).
   - `"residual_mean"`: The mean of the residuals (float).
   - `"residual_std"`: The standard deviation of the residuals (float).

Ensure your output matches standard floating-point precision. Run your script to generate `/home/user/results.json` before completing the task.
